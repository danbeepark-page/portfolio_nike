01 · MAU Growth — Finding Where the Users Are

Find
Cross-analyzed national study-abroad statistics (public data), K-campus user data, and search trends → identified countries with growing international student populations that K-campus was underreaching
Strategy
Selected 6 priority countries and analyzed each: acquisition channels, responsive keywords, content format preference, time-on-site, and retention. Designed localized GTM plans per market.
Execute
Ran country-specific integrated campaigns — concentrated budget on countries with high engagement but low paid coverage; applied locally tailored creatives across YouTube, TikTok, and Google
Result
New users in target countries grew 2x+ · MAU 450K · Monthly traffic 750K in 4 months

Designed content and funnel structure that naturally connects sign-up to deeper information access — leveraging high-intent content (scholarship guides, visa info) as conversion triggers
Execute
Ran 3-month intensive campaigns including ‘GKS 101’ — built interest through articles, AMAs, and webinars, then prompted sign-up at the moment of deepest engagement

Project 01

‘GKS 101’
Global Campaign

2025.12 – 2026.02 · Global Acquisition Campaign PM

01

Finding the Opportunity in Data

I pulled user-level data via GA4 and SQL to map where interest existed but reach didn’t. Cross-referencing keyword engagement patterns with country-level behavior revealed a cluster of markets with strong intent around the GKS scholarship — but with very low paid coverage. That gap became the campaign brief.

02

Designing the Touchpoint Between Target Segments and the Brand

I provided the information the target audience was looking for and connected that demand to the platform. GKS scholarship content was produced across multiple formats — editorial articles, scholar interviews, Reels, and community Q&As — while testing which formats and messages resonated most by country. I identified segment-specific channels (Reddit, Telegram, social) and ran localized plans per channel, while optimizing CPC and CVR across shared paid channels including Google, YouTube, and TikTok.

Live Event as Conversion Touchpoint

& Subscription Model

2020 – 2022 · E-Commerce Marketing · CRM Operations

01 · Problem

Visitors Came and Left — No Retention Loop

Korea JoongAng Daily had a readership of foreign readers and English learners, but lacked a structure that turned one-time visitors into returning readers. A persistent touchpoint was needed.

02 · Strategy

KakaoTalk as a Daily CRM Touchpoint

Built a KakaoTalk open chat channel delivering daily curated English news. Designed a flow where subscribers consumed content in-channel, then naturally clicked through to Korea JoongAng Daily when they wanted more.

03 · Subscription Model

Naver Smart Store Subscription Sales Model

Introduced a subscription-based sales model via Naver Smart Store — the first successful case among Korean media companies. Built a structure that shifted one-time buyers into long-term subscribers.
